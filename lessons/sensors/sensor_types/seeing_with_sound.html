How It Works

An ultrasonic sensor measures distance by sending out sound waves and listening for their echo. Think of it like shouting in a canyon‚Äîyou hear the echo bounce back!

  1. The sensor sends out a high-frequency sound wave.
  2. The wave travels through the air until it hits an object.
  3. The wave reflects back (echo), and the sensor detects it.
  4. The sensor calculates the distance based on how long it took the echo to return.
